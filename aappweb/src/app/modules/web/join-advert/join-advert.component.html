Membership Benefits

The American Association of Police Polygraphists (AAPP) consists of over 1200 law enforcement polygraph examiners and is professionally recognized throughout the United States of America as maintaining the highest standards of professional, moral and ethical conduct by assuming the responsibility for conduct and behavior designed to serve the cause of truth and justice.

Membership in the AAPP recognizes that the examiner has met licensing/certification requirements mandated in the state of residence/practice; and is a graduate of an AAPP recognized polygraph school. Members are able to apply and obtain a Forensic Examiner Certification which verifies the examiner is up to date and meets annual training requirements. AAPP members enjoy opportunities and forums for the exchange of information regarding polygraph experiences, studies and research. Members also enjoy membership pricing for annual training seminars.

Members can access to the National Office, staffed with a full-time Office Manager that is a clearing house for all polygraph related issues and is the primary custodian of all records and publications. AAPP members also have access to a vetted Directory of Membership resource, by names and/or geographic location, throughout the United States of America for law enforcement professional liaisons/use/contacts. A list of state association information and AAPP accredited polygraph school listings are also available.

AAPP members have access to vetted resources throughout the United States of America for law enforcement professional liasions/use/contacts.

AAPP members may take advantage of Internship Supervisory resources for new examiners; have access to Quality Control resources and AAPP “Active” members may run for office and serve on the committees and/or the AAPP Board.
